4th International Workshop on Collaboration in Knowledge Discovery and Decision-Making

Call for papers

In contemporary knowledge-based societies, knowledge constitutes a strategic resource for organizations, underpinning innovation, competitiveness, and decision-making. Beyond serving as a vehicle for progress, knowledge represents a form of organizational capital whose preservation, structuration, and effective use are essential for sustainable development and informed action.

Modern organizational transformations, such as outsourcing, decentralization, and workforce mobility, significantly increase the risk of knowledge loss, making knowledge retention and sharing critical challenges. When knowledge is poorly shared or fragmented, collaborative decision-making becomes increasingly difficult, often characterized by communication breakdowns among stakeholders. In such contexts, decision-makers are forced to negotiate consensus based on incomplete or asymmetric knowledge. Conversely, knowledge sharing fosters retention, collective understanding, and consensus-building, thereby enhancing both the quality and acceptance of decisions.

Our vision of collaborative decision-making extends beyond improving the early phases of the decision process, specifically intelligence and design, to also strengthening the acceptability, transparency, and legitimacy of decisions. When decisions are jointly constructed through shared knowledge and socio-technical systems, they are more likely to be understood, accepted, and effectively implemented. From this perspective, collaborative decision-making is not merely a procedural activity, but a knowledge-driven, socio-technical process supported by formal models, intelligent systems, and advanced AI techniques.

Shared decision-making plays a central role across many domains of human activity, including sustainability, agriculture, industry, governance, and public policy. In particular, sustainable development can be understood as a long-term decision-making strategy that requires integrating heterogeneous knowledge sources, stakeholder perspectives, and evolving constraints. Consequently, a key challenge lies in understanding how knowledge should be represented, formalized, shared, and operationalized to support collaborative decision-making effectively.

Decisioning 2026 is the fourth edition of the international workshop on Collaboration in Knowledge Discovery and Decision-Making. Organized by six research teams from Argentina, Chile, Colombia, France, and partner countries, the workshop aims to explore theoretical foundations, methodologies, and applications at the intersection of knowledge discovery, artificial intelligence, and decision support. The workshop explicitly encourages interaction between academia and industry, fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and practical impact.


Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

Knowledge and Decision Foundations

  • Knowledge discovery applications
  • Knowledge representation, modeling, and formalization
  • Knowledge elicitation, maintenance, and evolution
  • Knowledge-based systems and expert systems
  • Ontology engineering (modeling, mapping, alignment, integration)
  • Knowledge graphs and semantic technologies
  • Information and knowledge integration

Artificial Intelligence and Intelligent Systems

  • Artificial intelligence for decision-making
  • Language models for decisioning and decision support
  • Language models for reasoning and explainable inference
  • Neural network architectures for decision-making and knowledge processing
  • Hybrid and neuro-symbolic AI approaches
  • Decision support systems and intelligent assistants

Data, Text, and Information Processing

  • Data mining and data-driven decision support
  • Natural language processing
  • Text mining, classification, summarization, and semantic analysis
  • Information storage, annotation, and retrieval
  • Unstructured and semi-structured data management
  • Data simulation, modeling, visualization, and scenario analysis

Systems, Applications, and Sustainability

  • Content management and knowledge management systems
  • Workflow, process, and business process management systems
  • Decision-support and knowledge-driven information systems
  • Knowledge management for sustainability and long-term value creation
  • Sustainable decision-making and policy-oriented decision support
  • Knowledge applications in sustainability and agriculture
  • Knowledge representation and ontologies for sustainability and agriculture

Application Domains and Socio-Technical Systems

  • Knowledge-based systems for industrial systems and smart manufacturing
  • Decision-support systems for supply chains, logistics, and distribution networks
  • Knowledge-driven decision-making in networked systems and complex infrastructures
  • Applications in telecommunications and communication networks
  • Decision-support and knowledge modeling for energy systems and raw materials management
  • Knowledge-based approaches for financial systems, financial networks, and risk analysis
  • Decision-support systems for critical infrastructures and large-scale socio-technical systems
  • Cross-domain and multi-sector applications of knowledge-based decision-making systems

AI- and knowledge-based models, systems, and methodologies supporting decision-making in agro-industrial, agri-food, and environmental contexts

  • AI-driven decision support systems for agro-industrial and agri-food processes.
  • Applied machine learning and predictive analytics for agricultural and environmental decision-making.
  • Knowledge-based and data-driven decisioning in agri-food supply chains.
  • Integration of sensor data, climate models, and expert knowledge for agricultural decisions.
  • Decision support systems for irrigation management, climate risk assessment, yield forecasting, and logistics.
  • Digital twins and simulation-based decision support for agriculture and environmental systems.
